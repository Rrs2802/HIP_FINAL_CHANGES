Machine Learning Techniques For Acoustic Emission

Monitoring Of Hip Implants

This project focuses on improving hip implant monitoring by leveraging acoustic emission (AE) signals, providing real-time insights into implant condition. By applying machine learning techniques, a deep learning model is developed to analyze AE data and classify implant health, enabling early detection of deterioration. This proactive monitoring approach enhances the reliability of hip implants, reduces complications, and improves long-term patient outcomes, ultimately lessening the economic burden of implant-related issues.

Agenda

To develop a machine learning model that can use acoustic emission to determine the status of the hip joints, and to use machine learning techniques to analyze predictive maintenance of the hip joint problems.

Why Machine Learning Model?

Machine learning is essential in hip implant monitoring due to its ability to analyze complex acoustic emission (AE) data, distinguishing normal from abnormal patterns for early issue detection. ML techniques, including supervised and unsupervised learning, provide timely insights into hip implant conditions, ensuring their long-term functionality and patient well-being. Integration of ML with AE-based monitoring enhances early detection, predictive maintenance, and overall reliability of hip implants, contributing to the success of total hip replacement surgeries and improving patient outcomes.
